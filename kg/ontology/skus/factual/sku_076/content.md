## Emperor Jing of Han (孝景帝)

**Personal Name:** 刘启
**Mother:** Empress Dowager Dou
**Reign:** 157-141 BCE

**Rebellion of Seven States (七国之乱) - 154 BCE:**

**Rebel Kings:**
1. Liu Bi, King of Wu (吴王濞)
2. Liu Wu, King of Chu (楚王戊)
3. Liu Sui, King of Zhao (赵王遂)
4. Liu Ang, King of Jiaoxi (胶西王卬)
5. Liu Piguang, King of Jinan (济南王辟光)
6. Liu Xian, King of Zaichuan (菑川王贤)
7. Liu Xiongqu, King of Jiaodong (胶东王雄渠)

**Cause:** Chao Cuo's (晁错) policy of reducing feudal kingdoms' territories

**Resolution:**
- Emperor executed Chao Cuo to appease rebels (unsuccessful)
- General Dou Ying (窦婴) and Grand Commandant Zhou Yafu (周亚夫) led loyalist forces
- Rebellion suppressed within months

**Key Policies:**
- Reduced land tax to 1/30 of harvest (三十税一)
- Established Yangling mausoleum

**Succession:** Died in 141 BCE, succeeded by Crown Prince Liu Che (Emperor Wu)
