Chao Cuo (晁错), the Imperial Secretary (御史大夫), had advocated reducing the territories of the feudal kings. When the Seven States Rebellion began, Yuan Ang (袁盎) advised Emperor Jing that the rebels would disperse if Chao Cuo was executed. The Emperor agreed, saying he would not spare one man to appease the world. Chao Cuo was summoned by a captain, deceived into riding to the Eastern Market, and executed while still wearing his court robes. However, the rebellion continued despite his death.
