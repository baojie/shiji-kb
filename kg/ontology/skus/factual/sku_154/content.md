## Chen State Origins

Chen was ruled by descendants of Emperor Shun (虞帝舜). When Shun was a commoner, Emperor Yao gave him two daughters in marriage, and they lived at Gui Rui (妫汭). Their descendants took Gui (妫) as their clan name.

After Shun's death, the throne passed to Yu, but Shun's son Shang Jun (商均) was given a fief. During the Xia dynasty, the line was sometimes maintained, sometimes interrupted.

When King Wu conquered Yin, he sought out Shun's descendants and found Gui Man (妫满), enfeoffing him at Chen to maintain the sacrifices to Emperor Shun. This was Hu Gong (胡公), the first ruler of Chen.

The divination for Chen Li Gong's son Jing Zhong Wan (敬仲完) predicted that his descendants would flourish in another state - specifically a Jiang surname state (姜姓). Five generations later they would become prominent, and after eight generations, unequaled. This was fulfilled when the Chen family rose to power in Qi.
