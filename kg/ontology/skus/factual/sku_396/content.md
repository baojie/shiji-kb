**Protocol for Han Envoys:** According to Xiongnu law, Han envoys could only enter the royal tent if they removed their credentials (jie) and tattooed their faces with ink.

**Wang Wu:** A man from Beidi familiar with Hu customs, who removed his credentials and tattooed his face to enter the tent. The Chanyu favored him and made empty promises about sending his heir as hostage.

**Yang Xin:** A straightforward and stubborn man whom the Chanyu didn't trust. The Chanyu sat outside the tent to meet him. When Yang Xin demanded the Chanyu's heir as hostage, the Chanyu refused, citing the original agreement only required Han to send princesses and goods.

**Reciprocal Detention:** When Han detained Xiongnu envoys, the Xiongnu detained Han envoys in return. Over ten groups of Han envoys were detained at one point.

**Lu Chongguo:** Sent as an envoy with 2,000-shi rank insignia to escort a deceased Xiongnu noble's body. The Chanyu suspected Han had killed the noble and detained Lu for three years.

**Guo Ji:** Sent to inform the Chanyu that Nanyue's king's head hung at Han's northern gate. The Chanyu executed the protocol officer who introduced him and exiled Guo Ji to the northern sea.
