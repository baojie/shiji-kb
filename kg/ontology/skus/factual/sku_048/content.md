## Human Sacrifice in Qin

### First Recorded Instance
**Duke Wu (武公)** in his 20th year (678 BCE) was the first to practice human sacrifice (从死). **66 people** were buried with him.

### Duke Mu's Burial
**Duke Mu (穆公)** died in year 39 (621 BCE) and was buried at Yong (雍). **177 people** followed him in death. Among them were three good ministers from the Ziyu clan: **Yanxi (奄息), Zhonghang (仲行), and Zhenhu (针虎)**.

The people of Qin mourned them and composed the "Yellow Bird" poem (黄鸟之诗).

### Abolition
**Duke Xian (献公)** in his first year (384 BCE) **prohibited human sacrifice** (止从死), ending this practice.

### Historical Commentary
Sima Qian commented that Duke Mu expanded territory and dominated the west, but could not become the leader of the feudal lords because he abandoned his people at death by taking good ministers with him. This showed that Qin could not expand eastward thereafter.
