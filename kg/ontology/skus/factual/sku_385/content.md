**Annual Gatherings:**
- **First Month:** Minor gathering at the Chanyu's court for sacrifices
- **Fifth Month:** Major gathering at Longcheng (茏城) to worship ancestors, heaven, earth, and spirits
- **Autumn (when horses are fat):** Gathering at Dailin (蹛林) to count people and livestock

**Daily Rituals:** The Chanyu would bow to the rising sun in the morning and to the moon in the evening.

**Seating:** The senior sat on the left, facing north. The auspicious days were wu and ji (戊己).

**Warfare and Astronomy:** They observed the stars and moon before military actions. They attacked when the moon was full and retreated when it waned.

**Burial Customs:** They used coffins with gold, silver, clothing, and furs, but no burial mounds, trees, or mourning clothes. Favorite servants and concubines were sometimes killed to accompany the dead, sometimes numbering in the hundreds or thousands.
