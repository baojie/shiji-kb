When Qin besieged Handan, Pingyuan Lord went to Chu to seek alliance (合从). He needed 20 brave and capable men but only found 19 from his guests. Mao Sui (毛遂), who had been there for 3 years without recognition, volunteered. At Chu, negotiations stalled from sunrise to noon. Mao Sui confronted the Chu King with a sword, arguing that Chu should ally with Zhao against Qin, citing Qin's past humiliations of Chu (sacking Yanying, burning Yiling, desecrating Chu ancestors). The Chu King agreed to the alliance. Mao Sui had the King swear blood oath on the spot. Pingyuan Lord later praised Mao Sui, saying "With a three-inch tongue, Mao Sui proved stronger than a million troops" and declared he would never judge people again.
