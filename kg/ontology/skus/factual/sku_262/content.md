During Qin's siege of Handan, the city faced desperate conditions - people were cooking bones and exchanging children for food. Li Tong (李同), son of a hostel official, persuaded Pingyuan Lord to distribute his wealth to soldiers and have his family work alongside troops. This gained 3,000 death-defying soldiers. Li Tong led them against the Qin army, pushing them back 30 li. Li Tong died in battle, and his father was enfeoffed as Li Marquis (李侯). Chu's Spring Lord (春申君) and Wei's Xinling Lord (信陵君) arrived with reinforcements, and the siege was lifted.
