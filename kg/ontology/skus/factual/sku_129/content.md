## Notable Events in Qi History

### Yan Ying (晏婴/晏子)
- Served Duke Jing as advisor
- Famous for remonstrating against Duke Jing's extravagance
- When comet appeared (Year 32), advised that people's grievances cannot be dispelled by one person's prayers
- Died in Year 48 of Duke Jing's reign
- When Cui Zhu killed Duke Zhuang, Yan Ying famously said: "If the ruler dies for the state, his subjects should die with him. If he dies for himself, who among his private favorites would dare take responsibility?"

### The Historian Incident
- Grand Historian of Qi wrote "Cui Zhu killed Duke Zhuang"
- Cui Zhu killed him
- His brother wrote the same, Cui Zhu killed him too
- Youngest brother wrote the same, Cui Zhu let him live
- Demonstrates commitment to truthful recording of history

### Confucius at Qi
- Year 48 of Duke Jing: Met with Duke Ding of Lu at Jiagu (夹谷)
- Li Chu suggested using Lai people to create disturbance and capture Lu ruler
- Confucius ordered Lai people seized and executed
- Duke Jing, ashamed, returned Lu's seized territory

### Battle of An (鞍) - Duke Qing's 6th Year
- Xi Ke of Jin was insulted by Qi's duchess laughing at him
- Xi Ke vowed not to cross Yellow River again without revenge
- Led 800 chariots against Qi
- **Battle details**: Qi's driver Feng Choufu switched places with Duke Qing; Duke Qing escaped by pretending to fetch water
- Qi returned Lu and Wei's seized territory
- After defeat, Duke Qing relaxed hunting parks, reduced taxes, relieved the poor - people were pleased
