## Qu Yuan (屈原)

- **Name**: Ping (平)
- **Clan**: Same surname as the royal family of Chu (楚之同姓)
- **Position**: Left Minister (左徒) to King Huai of Chu (楚怀王)
- **Abilities**: Knowledgeable, skilled in governance and diplomacy, accomplished in rhetoric
- **Duties**: Advised the king on state affairs internally; received guests and handled diplomatic relations externally
- **Downfall**: Slandered by Shangguan Dafu (上官大夫) who was jealous of his abilities; King Huai became distant
- **Major Work**: Composed "Li Sao" (离骚) expressing his grievances
- **Death**: Committed suicide by drowning in the Miluo River (汨罗江) while holding a stone
- **Posthumous Influence**: Song Yu (宋玉), Tang Le (唐勒), and Jing Cha (景差) followed his literary style but did not dare to remonstrate directly
