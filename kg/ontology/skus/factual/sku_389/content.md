In the early Han dynasty, Han Xin (King of Dai) surrendered to the Xiongnu. The Xiongnu then attacked Taiyuan and reached Jinyang. Emperor Gaozu personally led an army against them.

**The Trap:** During a cold winter with heavy snow (soldiers lost fingers to frostbite at a rate of 2-3 per 10), Modu feigned defeat and retreat. The Han forces pursued with 320,000 men, mostly infantry. Emperor Gaozu arrived first at Pingcheng with his vanguard before the infantry caught up.

**The Siege:** Modu deployed 400,000 elite cavalry and surrounded Emperor Gaozu at Baideng for seven days. The Han forces inside and outside could not communicate or send supplies.

**Horse Colors by Direction:**
- West: all white horses
- East: all blue-grey horses
- North: all black horses
- South: all red-brown horses

**Escape:** The Emperor sent an envoy to secretly offer generous gifts to Modu's consort (Yanzhi). She persuaded Modu that the Han emperor had divine protection and that the Xiongnu couldn't occupy Han land anyway. Additionally, Modu's allies Wang Huang and Zhao Li failed to arrive as planned, making him suspect treachery. Modu opened one corner of the encirclement, and the Han forces escaped with arrows nocked and facing outward.
