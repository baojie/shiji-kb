## Chu Geographical Information

### Capitals
- **Danyang (丹阳)**: Initial capital, granted by King Cheng of Zhou
- **Ying (郢)**: Capital from King Wen's reign, captured by Qin in King Qingxiang Year 21
- **Ruo (鄀)**: Capital moved north after Year 12 of King Zhao
- **Shouchun (寿春)**: Final capital, renamed Ying, established in King Kaolie Year 22

### Key Territories
- **Jiang-Han region (江汉)**: Core territory between Yangtze and Han rivers
- **Chu lands measured at 1,000 li** during King Cheng's reign
- **5,000 li of territory** during King Qingxiang's reign
- **Hanzhong (汉中)**: Lost to Qin in Year 17 of King Huai
- **Shangyong (上庸)**: Ceded to Qin
- **Wu and Qianzhong commanderies (巫、黔中郡)**: Taken by Qin

### Strategic Locations
- **Pu (濮)**: Region opened and acquired during King Wu's reign
- **Chengpu (城濮)**: Site of Chu defeat by Jin
- **Yanling (鄢陵)**: Battle site where Jin defeated Chu
- **Wu Pass (武关)**: Where King Huai was captured by Qin
