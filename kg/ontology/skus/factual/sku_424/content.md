## Ji An (汲黯)
- **Origin**: Puyang; family served as ministers for seven generations
- **Character**: Arrogant, few formalities, direct criticism; studied Huang-Lao philosophy
- **Positions**: Crown Prince's attendant, Libationer, Central Grandee, Governor of Donghai, Chief Commandant of Nobles
- **Governance**: Preferred non-action; delegated to subordinates; Donghai well-governed within a year
- **Famous quote**: Told Emperor Wu "Your Majesty has many desires internally but practices benevolence externally - how can you hope to emulate the governance of Yao and Shun?"
- **Relationship with Emperor**: Emperor would not see him without proper attire; respected his integrity
- **Conflict**: Opposed Zhang Tang and Gongsun Hong; called them "petty officials"
- **Later years**: Made Governor of Huaiyang; died after seven years
