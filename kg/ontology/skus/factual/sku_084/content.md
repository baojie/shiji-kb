Qin began as a small, remote state, considered by the Central States to be no different from the Rong and Di barbarians. Duke Xiang of Qin was first enfeoffed as a feudal lord after the Quanrong defeated King You, causing the Zhou to move east to Luoyi. Duke Wen crossed the Long mountains, expelled the Yi and Di, and established himself between Qi and Yong. Duke Mu reformed governance and extended the eastern border to the Yellow River, becoming comparable to Duke Huan of Qi and Duke Wen of Jin. After Duke Xian, Qin consistently dominated the feudal lords. Though Qin's virtue and righteousness were inferior to the violence of Lu and Wei, and its military strength less than the Three Jins, it ultimately unified the world - attributed by some to heavenly assistance.
