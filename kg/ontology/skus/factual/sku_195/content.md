## The Fall of Wei

**Year 3 of King Jia:**
- Qin flooded Daliang by diverting the Yellow River
- After three months, the city walls collapsed
- King Jia surrendered
- Wei was destroyed and made into Qin commanderies and counties

**The Grand Historian's Account:**
The Grand Historian visited the ruins of old Daliang. People there said: "When Qin broke Liang, they diverted the river canals to flood Daliang. After three months the city walls broke, the King asked to surrender, and Wei was destroyed."

**Historian's Commentary:**
Commentators say Wei was destroyed because it did not employ Lord Xinling, leading to national weakness. The Grand Historian disagreed: "Heaven was just letting Qin pacify the realm. Their enterprise was not yet complete. Even if Wei had obtained an assistant like Ah Heng, what benefit would it have been?"
