## Chu Ancestral Origins

Chu's ancestors descended from Emperor Zhuanxu Gaoyang (帝颛顼高阳).

**Lineage:**
- **Gaoyang (高阳)**: Grandson of the Yellow Emperor (黄帝), son of Changyi (昌意)
- Gaoyang → Cheng (称) → Juanzhang (卷章) → Zhongli (重黎)

**Zhongli (重黎)**: Served as Fire Official (火正) under Emperor Ku, achieved great merit, was named Zhurong (祝融). Was executed by Emperor Ku for failing to completely suppress the Gonggong (共工) rebellion. His brother Wuhui (吴回) succeeded him as Zhurong.

**Wuhui → Luzhong (陆终)**: Luzhong had six sons, born by caesarean section:
1. Kunwu (昆吾) - became a feudal lord during Xia, destroyed by Tang of Shang during Jie's reign
2. Canhu (参胡)
3. Pengzu (彭祖) - became a feudal lord during Yin, destroyed in late Yin period
4. Huiren (会人)
5. Caoxing (曹姓)
6. **Jilian (季连)** - surname Mi (琇), ancestor of Chu

**Jilian → Fujiu (附沮) → Xue Xiong (穴熊)**: The lineage declined afterward, some remaining in central China, others among the barbarian tribes.

**Yuxiong (鬻熊)**: Descendant of Jilian during King Wen of Zhou's time. Served King Wen but died early. His son was Xiong Li (熊丽) → Xiong Kuang (熊狂) → Xiong Yi (熊绎).
