## 周公旦 (Zhou Gong Dan / Duke of Zhou)

**Identity:** Younger brother of King Wu of Zhou (周武王弟), son of King Wen (文王)

**Character:** Known for filial piety and benevolence, distinguished among his siblings

**Key Roles:**
- Assisted King Wu in military campaigns including the battle of Muye (牧野)
- Authored the "Mu Shi" (牧誓 - Oath at Muye)
- Held the large axe (大钺) alongside Duke of Shao holding the small axe during ceremonies
- Founded the state of Lu (鲁), enfeoffed at Qufu (曲阜), the former capital of Shaohao (少昊之虚)
- Did not take up residence in Lu, remained to assist King Wu

**Regency:**
- After King Wu's death, served as regent for young King Cheng (成王)
- Faced accusations from Guan Shu and other brothers of being disloyal
- Suppressed rebellions by Guan, Cai, and Wu Geng, along with Huai Yi tribes
- Established Kang Shu at Wei (卫) and Wei Zi at Song (宋)
- Returned governance to King Cheng after 7 years of regency

**Death:** Requested burial at Cheng Zhou to show loyalty; King Cheng buried him at Bi (毕) near King Wen's tomb

**Posthumous Honors:** Lu was granted the right to perform suburban sacrifices to King Wen and use royal ritual music
