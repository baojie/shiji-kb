Zhou Yafu followed the advice of Deng, a former guest of his father: He fortified his position at Changyi (昌邑), allowing the Liang kingdom to bear the brunt of Wu's attack. He sent light troops to cut off Wu's supply lines at the Huai-Si river crossing. When Wu's forces ran out of food and became desperate, they attempted a night raid. Zhou Yafu, anticipating this, had his troops prepare for an attack from the northwest when the Wu forces feinted from the southeast. The Wu army was crushed, with many soldiers starving to death.
