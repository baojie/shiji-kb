**Weapons:** Long-range weapons were bows and arrows; short-range weapons were swords and spears.

**Tactics:** They advanced when there was advantage and retreated when there was none; they did not consider retreat shameful. They acted according to where benefit lay, without regard for ritual propriety.

**Military Training:** Children could ride sheep and shoot birds and mice with bows. As they grew older, they shot foxes and rabbits for food. Adults capable of drawing bows all served as armored cavalry.

**Combat Behavior:** In peacetime, they followed their herds and hunted for a living. In times of crisis, they practiced warfare and invaded - this was their nature. In battle, those who carried back the dead received all the deceased's family property.
