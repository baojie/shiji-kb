**Imperial Title Creation (221 BCE)**
- Created title "Emperor" (皇帝), combining "Huang" (皇) from ancient times and "Di" (帝)
- Emperor's commands called "Zhi" (制), orders called "Zhao" (诏)
- Emperor's self-reference: "Zhen" (朕)
- Posthumous titles abolished; succession numbered (First Emperor, Second Emperor, etc.)

**Five Virtues System**
- Qin adopted Water Virtue (水德), believing it succeeded Zhou's Fire Virtue
- Calendar changed: year began with 10th month
- Color preference: Black (upper)
- Sacred number: Six (6 cun seals, 6 chi carriage width, 6 horses per carriage)
- Yellow River renamed "Virtue River" (德水)

**Administrative Division**
- Empire divided into 36 commanderies (郡)
- Each commandery had: Governor (守), Commandant (尉), Inspector (监)
- People renamed "Qianshou" (黔首 - black-headed ones)

**Standardization Measures**
- Unified weights and measures
- Standardized carriage axle width (same gauge)
- Unified writing system
- Weapons confiscated and melted into 12 bronze statues (each 1000 shi weight)
- 120,000 wealthy families relocated to Xianyang
