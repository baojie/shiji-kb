## The 300 Horse-Eaters of Duke Mu

When Duke Mu (穆公) lost his fine horses, over 300 common people from the area beneath Qi Mountain caught and ate them. Officials captured them and wanted to punish them by law.

Duke Mu said: "A gentleman does not harm people over livestock. I have heard that eating fine horse meat without drinking alcohol harms the body." He then gave them all wine and pardoned them.

Later, at the Battle of Han (韩地), Duke Mu was surrounded by Jin forces and wounded. The 300 men who had eaten the horses charged into the Jin army, broke the encirclement, and rescued Duke Mu. They also captured the Jin ruler Duke Hui (晋惠公夷吾).

This story illustrates Duke Mu's virtue and the loyalty it inspired.
