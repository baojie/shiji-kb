## Shang Yang Reforms (商鞅变法)

### Timeline
- **Year 3 of Duke Xiao**: Shang Yang (Wei Yang) proposed reforms
- **Year 10**: Shang Yang appointed Grand Liangzao (大良造)
- **Year 12**: Capital moved to Xianyang (咸阳)

### Key Reforms
1. **Administrative**: Consolidated small settlements into large counties (41 counties total), each with one magistrate
2. **Land**: Opened up field paths (阡陌), reformed land system
3. **Legal**: Established strict rewards and punishments
4. **Military**: Rewards for military achievements
5. **Economic**: Encouraged agriculture and weaving

### Outcome
- Initially, people suffered; after 3 years, people found it convenient
- Qin became well-governed and powerful
- Shang Yang titled Lord of Shang (商君)

### Death of Shang Yang
After Duke Xiao died, the crown prince (King Hui Wen) had Shang Yang executed by being torn apart by chariots (车裂), as the crown prince's tutor had been punished under Shang Yang's laws.
