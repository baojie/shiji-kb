## Jia Yi (贾谊/贾生)

- **Origin**: Luoyang (雒阳)
- **Early Recognition**: At age 18, known for reciting poetry and writing
- **Career under Emperor Wen of Han**:
  - Appointed as Erudite (博士)
  - At age 20+, was the youngest among scholars
  - Promoted to Taizhong Dafu (太中大夫) within one year
- **Reform Proposals**: Change calendar, clothing colors (yellow preferred), institutional reforms, official titles, ritual music
- **Opposition**: Slandered by Jiang (绛), Guan (灌), Dongyang Hou, and Feng Jing as "young and inexperienced, wanting to monopolize power"
- **Exile**: Appointed Grand Tutor to the King of Changsha (长沙王太傅)
- **Literary Works**: "Fu on Owl" (服乌赋), "Lament for Qu Yuan" (吊屈原赋)
- **Later Position**: Grand Tutor to Prince Huai of Liang (梁怀王太傅)
- **Death**: Died at age 33, grieving after Prince Huai fell from a horse and died
- **Descendants**: Two grandsons became commandery governors; Jia Jia (贾嘉) maintained family scholarly tradition, reached Nine Ministers rank under Emperor Zhao
