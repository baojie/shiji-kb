## Aftermath of Zhou's Conquest of Yin

After conquering Yin, King Wu:
1. Enfeoffed Wu Geng Lu Fu (武庚禄父), son of King Zhou, with the remaining Yin people to continue Yin sacrifices
2. Appointed Guan Shu and Cai Shu to supervise Wu Geng
3. Enfeoffed his brothers: Guan Shu at Guan, Cai Shu at Cai, Zhou Gong Dan at Lu (as regent), Cao Shu Zhenduo at Cao, Cheng Shu Wu at Cheng, Huo Shu Chu at Huo
4. Kang Shu Feng and Yan Ji Zai were too young and not yet enfeoffed

After King Wu's death, with King Cheng young, the Duke of Zhou (Zhou Gong Dan) acted as regent. Guan Shu and Cai Shu suspected him of acting against King Cheng's interests and, allying with Wu Geng, rebelled.

The Duke of Zhou, under King Cheng's authority:
- Executed Wu Geng and Guan Shu
- Exiled Cai Shu (with 10 chariots and 70 followers)
- Divided the remaining Yin people: one group given to Wei Zi Qi at Song to continue Yin sacrifices; another to Kang Shu as ruler of Wei (Wei Kang Shu)
- Enfeoffed Yan Ji Zai at Yan

Kang Shu and Yan Ji Zai both served well and were appointed Minister of Justice (司寇) and Minister of Works (司空) respectively.
