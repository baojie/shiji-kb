## Economic Philosophy
- **Laozi's ideal**: Small communities with no interaction between neighbors
- **Sima Qian's view**: People naturally pursue wealth; governance should work with this tendency
- **Methods of governance**: 1) Follow nature, 2) Guide with profit, 3) Teach, 4) Regulate, 5) Compete (worst)

## Regional Products
- **Shanxi**: Timber, bamboo, grain, hemp, yak tails, jade
- **Shandong**: Fish, salt, lacquer, silk, entertainment
- **Jiangnan**: Catalpa, ginger, cinnamon, gold, tin, cinnabar, rhinoceros horn, tortoise shell, pearls
- **North of Longmen-Jieshi**: Horses, cattle, sheep, furs, sinew, horn

## Famous Wealthy Individuals

### Fan Li (Tao Zhu Gong)
- **Origin**: Served King Goujian of Yue
- **Method**: Applied Ji Ran's economic principles; "buy cheap, sell dear"
- **Achievement**: Made and lost three fortunes of 1,000 gold; gave to poor friends

### Bai Gui
- **Method**: "When others discard, I take; when others take, I give"
- **Principles**: Required wisdom, courage, benevolence, and strength

### Zhuo family (Shu)
- **Origin**: Zhao iron smelters
- **Relocation**: Moved to Linqiong after Qin conquered Zhao
- **Wealth**: 1,000 servants; wealth rivaled princes

### Widow Qing of Ba
- **Source**: Cinnabar mines for generations
- **Status**: Emperor Qin built "Tower of the Chaste Widow Qing" in her honor

### Wusalt family (Wu Yan)
- **Method**: Lent 1,000 gold to marquises during Wu-Chu rebellion at 10x interest
- **Result**: Wealth rivaled all of Guanzhong within one year
