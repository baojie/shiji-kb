**Mayi Ambush (133 BCE):** Han attempted to lure the Chanyu into an ambush at Mayi with 300,000 troops. A captured Han officer revealed the plot, and the Chanyu withdrew. This ended the Heqin policy.

**Campaign of 129-128 BCE:**
- Wei Qing: 700 heads captured at Longcheng
- Gongsun He: no results
- Gongsun Ao: lost 7,000 men
- Li Guang: captured but escaped

**Campaign of 127 BCE (Yuanshuo 2):** Wei Qing attacked Loufan and Baiyang kings in Henan, capturing thousands of heads and over a million cattle and sheep. Han established Shuofang commandery.

**Campaign of 124 BCE:** Wei Qing with 100,000+ troops attacked the Right Wise King 600-700 li beyond the border, capturing 15,000 people and 10+ minor kings.

**Campaign of 121 BCE:**
- Huo Qubing: 18,000 heads captured west of Yanzhi Mountain, captured the Golden Man used by King Xiutu for heaven worship
- Later campaign: 30,000+ heads captured at Qilian Mountain, 70+ minor kings captured
- Xiutu and Hunye kings surrendered with 40,000 people (claimed 100,000)

**Campaign of 119 BCE:**
- Wei Qing: 19,000 heads captured, reached Zhaoxin City at Tianyan Mountain
- Huo Qubing: 70,000+ heads captured, performed Feng sacrifice at Langjuxu Mountain and Shan sacrifice at Guyan Mountain, reached the Hanhai (Lake Baikal)

**Aftermath:** The Xiongnu fled far north, and there was no royal court south of the Gobi desert.
