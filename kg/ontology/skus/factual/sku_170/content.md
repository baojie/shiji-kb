## Major Historical Events in Chu History

### Founding and Early Period
- **Enfeoffment**: Xiong Yi enfeoffed as Viscount of Chu by King Cheng of Zhou
- **First Royal Title**: King Wu (Xiong Tong) first declared himself King in Year 37, when Zhou refused to elevate his title

### Key Battles
- **Battle of Chengpu (城濮之战)**: Year 39 of King Cheng - Chu defeated by Jin led by Duke Wen (Chong'er)
- **Battle of Yanling (鄢陵之战)**: Year 16 of King Gong - Chu defeated by Jin, King wounded in eye
- **Battle of Danyang (丹阳之战)**: Year 17 of King Huai - catastrophic defeat by Qin, 80,000 killed
- **Fall of Ying (郢)**: Year 21 of King Qingxiang - Qin general Bai Qi captured capital, burned royal tombs

### Political Intrigues
- **King Cheng's death**: Killed by son Shangchen in coup after attempting to change heir
- **King Ling's downfall**: Overthrown after 12 years, starved to death in wilderness
- **Fei Wuji affair**: Slander led to Crown Prince Jian's flight, Wu She and Wu Shang's execution, Wu Zixu's flight to Wu
- **Bai Gong Rebellion**: Year 8 of King Hui - Bai Gong killed Lingyin, briefly seized throne

### External Relations
- **Zhou recognition**: King Cheng received sacrificial meat from Zhou, told to control southern barbarians
- **Six States Alliance**: King Huai served as leader of alliance against Qin
- **Zhang Yi's deception**: King Huai deceived into breaking with Qi for promised 600 li that became 6 li
- **King Huai's capture**: Lured to Qin under pretense of alliance, held captive until death

### Fall of Chu
- **Year 5 of Fuchu**: Qin generals Wang Jian and Meng Wu conquered Chu
- King Fuchu captured, Chu annexed as Qin commandery
