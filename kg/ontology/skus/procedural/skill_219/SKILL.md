# Military Justice Deliberation Process

## Overview
A structured decision-making procedure for handling defeated generals who return to camp after losing their forces. Based on Wei Qing's handling of General Su Jian.

## Steps

1. **Receive the Returning Officer**
   - Allow the defeated commander to return and report
   - Do not immediately execute or punish

2. **Convene a Council of Advisors**
   - Gather legal officers (正), secretaries (长史), and advisors (议郎)
   - Present the case for deliberation

3. **Hear Competing Arguments**
   - **Strict View**: Argue for execution to establish authority and deterrence
   - **Lenient View**: Argue that the officer fought bravely against overwhelming odds and returned out of loyalty

4. **Consider Key Factors**
   - Circumstances of defeat (outnumbered, fought valiantly)
   - Officer's loyalty (returned rather than defected)
   - Precedent and impact on morale

5. **Defer to Higher Authority**
   - As a field commander, avoid unilateral execution of high-ranking officers
   - Escort the accused to the sovereign for final judgment
   - This demonstrates respect for hierarchy and avoids overstepping authority

## Decision Principle
"不患无威" - A true leader need not worry about lacking authority; executing subordinates to demonstrate power is unnecessary and may undermine trust.

## Expected Outcome
The sovereign makes the final decision, preserving chain of command while allowing for mercy or justice as appropriate.
