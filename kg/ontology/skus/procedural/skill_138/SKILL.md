# Military Discipline Establishment (from Sima Rangju and Sun Wu)

## Overview
Methods for establishing military authority through strict, impartial enforcement of rules.

## Method 1: Enforcing Punctuality

### Steps
1. Set a clear meeting time (e.g., noon at the camp gate)
2. Arrive early yourself and set up timing devices
3. When the appointed person arrives late:
   - Ask for explanation
   - Reject excuses (even legitimate ones like "seeing off relatives")
   - State principle: "On receiving orders, forget family; at camp, forget relatives; in battle, forget self"
4. Consult military law for the penalty
5. Execute the penalty publicly
6. If ruler sends pardon, respond: "When general is in camp, some ruler's orders are not accepted"
7. Apply penalty to a substitute (e.g., execute the messenger's servant, chariot driver, or horse)

## Method 2: Training with Unconventional Subjects

### Steps
1. Accept any subjects for training demonstration (even palace women)
2. Divide into units with clear leaders
3. Explain commands clearly (left, right, forward, back)
4. Give initial commands and observe
5. If subjects laugh and don't comply:
   - First time: Accept blame for unclear instructions
   - Restate commands clearly
6. If subjects still don't comply:
   - State: "Instructions were clear; failure to obey is the soldiers' fault"
   - Execute the unit leaders publicly
7. Continue training with new leaders
8. Result: Perfect compliance

## Key Principle
"将在军，君令有所不受" - When the general is in the field, he need not accept all commands from the ruler.

## Expected Outcome
An army that follows orders precisely and views the commander with awe.
