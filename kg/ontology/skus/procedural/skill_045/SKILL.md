# Imperial Legitimacy Ritual (封禅礼仪)

## Overview
A procedure for performing the Feng and Shan sacrifices to establish and reinforce imperial legitimacy through connection to divine authority.

## Prerequisites
- Unification of the realm
- Period of peace and prosperity
- Auspicious signs or omens
- Proper ritual vessels and offerings

## Steps

1. **Preparation Phase**
   - Consult with ritual specialists about proper procedures
   - Gather appropriate offerings and vessels
   - Select auspicious dates
   - Prepare the ritual sites at Mount Tai

2. **The Feng Sacrifice (封) - At Mount Tai Summit**
   - Ascend Mount Tai
   - Create a ritual platform facing east
   - Bury jade tablets with secret prayers
   - Offer sacrifices to Heaven
   - Use yellow offerings and vessels

3. **The Shan Sacrifice (禅) - At Foot of Mount Tai**
   - Descend to the northeast foot of Mount Tai
   - Perform sacrifices to Earth at Suran Mountain
   - Use appropriate offerings for Earth deities

4. **Announcement and Celebration**
   - Proclaim the completion of rituals
   - Grant amnesty and rewards to subjects
   - Reduce taxes in affected regions
   - Commemorate with inscriptions

## Key Principles
- Secrecy of the ritual content is essential
- Proper sequence must be maintained
- Connection between Heaven, Earth, and Emperor must be demonstrated

## Expected Outcomes
- Divine endorsement of imperial rule
- Enhanced legitimacy in eyes of subjects
- Historical record of achievement

## Historical Context
Emperor Wu of Han performed the Feng and Shan sacrifices to establish his legitimacy as a ruler who had unified the realm and brought prosperity.
