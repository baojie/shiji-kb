# Evaluating Peace Negotiations

## Overview
A framework for determining whether entering peace negotiations will succeed or merely signal weakness.

## Analysis Steps

1. **Assess adversary's goal** - Are they seeking victory or willing to settle?
2. **Identify who controls negotiations** - The stronger party sets the terms
3. **Predict adversary's behavior** - Will they use negotiations for propaganda?
4. **Evaluate third-party perception** - Will allies abandon you if they think you're negotiating separately?
5. **Consider the signaling effect** - Seeking peace may indicate weakness and invite more aggression

## Warning Signs Negotiations Will Fail
- Adversary is fully committed to victory
- Adversory will publicize negotiations to isolate you from allies
- Adversary has no incentive to offer good terms
- Your allies will abandon you if they learn of negotiations

## Alternative Approach
Instead of direct negotiation:
1. Approach potential allies with offers
2. Create counter-alliance pressure
3. Let adversary seek peace from fear of coalition

## Expected Outcome
Avoiding failed negotiations that isolate you and strengthen the adversary
