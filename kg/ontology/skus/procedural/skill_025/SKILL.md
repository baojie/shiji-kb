# Respond to Military Defeat

## Overview
A procedure for leaders to respond to military failures, take responsibility, and rebuild for future success. Based on Duke Mu of Qin's response to the Xiao defeat.

## Steps

1. **Acknowledge the Defeat**
   - Accept the loss without deflecting blame
   - Secure release of captured commanders if possible

2. **Take Personal Responsibility**
   - Admit failure to follow wise counsel
   - Do not blame subordinates for the defeat

3. **Honor Returning Commanders**
   - Meet defeated commanders with respect
   - Wear mourning clothes to show solidarity
   - Weep with them over the loss

4. **Restore Positions**
   - Return commanders to their former ranks
   - Increase support and resources for them

5. **Issue Public Acknowledgment**
   - Make a formal declaration accepting fault
   - Record the lesson for future generations

6. **Rebuild and Retry**
   - Strengthen forces over time
   - Launch renewed campaign when ready
   - Use symbolic acts (burning boats) to demonstrate commitment

7. **Commemorate the Fallen**
   - Properly bury the dead
   - Conduct mourning ceremonies
   - Honor their sacrifice publicly

## Decision Points
- If defeat came from ignoring advice, explicitly acknowledge this
- If commanders fought bravely despite defeat, reward their loyalty

## Expected Outcomes
- Maintained loyalty of military commanders
- Enhanced leader's reputation for accountability
- Eventual military success through persistence
