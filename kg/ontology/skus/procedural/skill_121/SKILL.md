# Negotiation Under Imminent Threat

A strategy for negotiating when facing execution, using leverage and conditional agreements.

## Overview
When facing imminent execution at the request of a third party offering payment/territory, restructure the transaction to create mutual benefit and reduce risk for all parties.

## Steps

1. **Reframe the Transaction**
   - Argue that a living hostage has more value than a dead one
   - "与其以死痤市，不如以生痤市" (Better to trade with a living person than a dead one)

2. **Identify the Risk for the Executing Authority**
   - What if the third party doesn't deliver after the execution?
   - The executing authority loses both the hostage and the payment

3. **Propose a Sequenced Agreement**
   - Secure the payment/territory first
   - Then proceed with the execution
   - This protects the executing authority's interests

4. **Seek Additional Advocacy**
   - Contact influential allies who can intervene
   - Present broader strategic implications of the precedent being set

## Decision Point
If a powerful third party might make similar demands in the future, influential allies have reason to prevent this precedent.

## Expected Outcome
The executing authority agrees to secure payment first, creating opportunity for intervention or escape while the transaction is being negotiated.
