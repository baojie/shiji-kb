## Duke Huan's Hegemony (霸业)

### Domestic Reforms
- Organized military by linking five families (连五家之兵)
- Developed fish and salt industries
- Relieved the poor (以赡贫穷)
- Employed the worthy (禄贤能)

### Military Campaigns
| Year | Event |
|------|------|
| Year 2 | Destroyed Tan (郯); Tan ruler fled to Ju |
| Year 5 | Attacked Lu; made treaty at Ke (柯) |
| Year 7 | Feudal lords met at Zhen (甄); Duke Huan first became hegemon |
| Year 14 | Chen Li Gong's son Wan (Jing Zhong) fled to Qi |
| Year 23 | Saved Yan from Shanrong attack; pursued to Guzhu |
| Year 27 | Killed Ai Jiang (his sister) who was involved in Lu's chaos |
| Year 28 | Led feudal lords to build city at Chuqiu for Wei |
| Year 29 | Attacked Cai due to personal dispute over Cai Ji |
| Year 30 | Attacked Chu; demanded tribute of "wrapping grass" (包茅) and asked about King Zhao's disappearance; made treaty at Shaoling (召陵) |
| Year 35 | Meeting at Kuiqiu (葵丘); received royal gifts from King Xiang of Zhou |
| Year 38 | Sent Guan Zhong to mediate between Zhou and Rong/Di |
| Year 42 | Led feudal lords to defend Zhou against Rong attack |

### Famous Incident: Cao Mo's Assassination Attempt
At treaty meeting at Ke, Lu general Cao Mo held Duke Huan at knifepoint on the altar, demanding return of Lu's seized territory. Despite later regret, Duke Huan honored the promise under Guan Zhong's advice, gaining trust of all feudal lords.

### Duke Huan's Boast (Year 35)
"I campaigned south to Shaoling viewing Xiong Mountain; north against Shanrong, Lizhi, Guzhu; west against Daxia crossing the desert; climbed Taihang with bound horses and suspended chariots to Beier Mountain. I had three military meetings and six chariot meetings of feudal lords, nine gatherings uniting the lords, one correction of the world."

### Attempted Feng and Shan Sacrifices
- Duke Huan wanted to perform Feng sacrifice at Mount Tai and Shan sacrifice at Liangfu
- Guan Zhong dissuaded him by saying rare auspicious creatures must appear first
- Duke Huan abandoned the plan
