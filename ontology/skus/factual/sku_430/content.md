## Ji Ru and Hong Ru
- **Era**: Emperors Gaozu and Hui
- **Method**: Gained favor through flattery and beauty; slept with emperors
- **Influence**: Officials sought their intercession; courtiers imitated their style of dress and cosmetics

## Deng Tong (邓通)
- **Origin**: Shu, Nan'an
- **Position**: Yellow-head boatman
- **Dream**: Emperor Wen dreamed a boatman pushed him to heaven; found Deng Tong matched the description
- **Wealth**: Given copper mountain in Yan Dao to mint coins; "Deng coins" circulated throughout empire
- **Service**: Sucked pus from emperor's boil; emperor tested crown prince who showed reluctance
- **Downfall**: After Emperor Wen's death, accused of illegal minting; all property confiscated; died penniless in another's home

## Han Yan (韩嫣)
- **Origin**: Grandson of Marquis of Bowang
- **Relationship**: Studied with Emperor Wu when he was Prince of Jiaodong
- **Position**: Grand Master; rewarded like Deng Tong
- **Death**: Empress Dowager Wang ordered his execution for impropriety with palace women

## Li Yannian (李延年)
- **Origin**: Zhongshan
- **Background**: Entertainer family; castrated for crime
- **Talent**: Created new music; sister was Emperor Wu's favorite concubine
- **Position**: Grand Master with 2,000-picul salary
- **Death**: Executed with brothers after sister's death
