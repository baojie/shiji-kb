**Jixia Scholars (稷下先生)**

**Chunyu Kun (淳于髡)**
- Origin: Qi
- Known for: Broad knowledge, strong memory, studied without specializing
- Style: Modeled after Yan Ying; skilled at reading intentions and expressions
- Anecdote: Remained silent when meeting King Hui of Liang twice because king was distracted by horses and music
- Later: Spoke for 3 days and nights without fatigue; declined offer of chancellor position

**Shen Dao (慎到)** - From Zhao, wrote 12 treatises
**Tian Pian (田骈)** - From Qi
**Jie Zi (接子)** - From Qi
**Huan Yuan (环渊)** - From Chu, wrote upper and lower chapters
**Zou Shi (驺奭)** - From Qi, adopted Zou Yan's methods

All studied Huang-Lao Daoist methods. King of Qi honored them as Lie Dafu (列大夫), built residences on main thoroughfares with high gates and large houses.
