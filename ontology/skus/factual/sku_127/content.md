## Rise of Tian (Chen) Family in Qi

### Origins
- **Chen Wan (陈完)**, posthumous name Jing Zhong (敬仲), fled from Chen to Qi in Duke Huan's 14th year
- Duke Huan wanted to make him minister; he declined and became Gongzheng (工正, director of works)
- Changed surname to Tian (田)
- Ancestor of Tian Chengzi Chang (田成子常)

### Key Events in Tian Family Rise

**Duke Jing's Era (Year 9)**:
- Yan Ying told Shuxiang: "Qi's government will eventually belong to the Tian family. Though lacking great virtue, they use public power for private benefit, winning the people's love."

**Duke Jing's Era (Year 55)**:
- Fan and Zhongxing rebelled in Jin, requested grain from Qi
- **Tian Qi (田乞)** persuaded Duke Jing to send grain, building alliances with rebellious ministers

**After Duke Jing's Death**:
- Tian Qi falsely served Gao and Guo families while plotting against them
- Attacked and killed Gao Zhaozi; Guo Huizi fled to Ju
- Recalled Prince Yangsheng from Lu, hid him in a sack
- At a banquet, revealed Yangsheng and proclaimed him ruler (Duke Dao)
- Killed young ruler Yan Ruzi

**Duke Jian's Era**:
- Jian Zhi (监止) was favored and held power
- Tian Chengzi (田成子) feared him
- **Year 4**: Tian family launched coup, killed Jian Zhi
- **Tian Chang (田常)** captured and killed Duke Jian at Quzhou

**Duke Ping's Era**:
- Tian Chang served as chancellor, monopolized Qi government
- Carved Qi's land east of Anping as Tian family fief

**Duke Kang's Era (Year 19)**:
- **Tian He (田和)**, great-grandson of Tian Chang, became feudal lord
- Moved Duke Kang to the seaside

**Duke Kang's Era (Year 26)**:
- Duke Kang died
- Lü (Jiang) clan's sacrifices ended
- Tian family completely possessed Qi
- Became **Duke Wei of Qi (齐威王)**, powerful in the world
