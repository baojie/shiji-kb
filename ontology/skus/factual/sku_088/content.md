Confucius, after failing to be employed by over 70 rulers, traveled to the Zhou court, examined historical records and old traditions, and compiled the Spring and Autumn Annals in Lu. The work covers from Duke Yin to the capture of the lin (phases from 722-481 BCE). He condensed the text, removed redundancies, and established principles. The 70 disciples transmitted the meaning orally, as the text contained criticism and praise that could not be written down. Zuo Qiuming, fearing the disciples would each interpret differently and lose the true meaning, compiled the Zuo Zhuan. Other works derived from Spring and Autumn include: Duo Jiao's 'Duo Shi Wei' (40 chapters) for King Wei of Chu; Yu Qing's 'Yu Shi Chun Qiu' (8 chapters); Lü Buwei's 'Lü Shi Chun Qiu' (8 views, 6 discussions, 12 records). Scholars like Xun Qing, Mencius, Gongsun Gu, and Han Fei also cited the Spring and Autumn in their writings.
