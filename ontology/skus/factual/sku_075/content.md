## Emperor Wen of Han (孝文帝)

**Personal Name:** 刘恒
**Parentage:** Son of Gaozu and Consort Bo
**Previous Title:** King of Dai
**Reign:** 180-157 BCE (23 years)

**Key Reforms:**

1. **Abolition of Collective Punishment (除收帑诸相坐律令):**
   - Previously, family members were punished for one person's crimes
   - Emperor Wen abolished this practice

2. **Abolition of Corporal Punishment (除肉刑):**
   - Inspired by Tiying (缇萦), daughter of Chunyu Gong
   - Abolished punishments that mutilated the body (cutting off nose, feet, castration)

3. **Agricultural Policy:**
   - Personally plowed the ceremonial field (籍田)
   - Abolished land taxes

4. **Frugality:**
   - Did not expand palaces, gardens, or stables
   - Once considered building a terrace but abandoned it when told it would cost 100 gold (equivalent to 10 middle-class families' wealth)
   - Wore simple silk clothes
   - Ordered his mausoleum (Baling) to use only pottery, no gold, silver, bronze, or tin decorations

5. **Legal Reforms:**
   - Abolished the crime of defamation (诽谤妖言之罪)
   - Allowed officials to speak freely about governance

**Foreign Policy:**
- Maintained peace with Xiongnu through heqin (marriage alliances)
- Did not launch deep punitive expeditions to avoid burdening the people

**Death:** Died in 157 BCE, buried at Baling
