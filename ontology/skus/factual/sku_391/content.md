Zhonghang Yue (中行说) was a eunuch from Yan sent to accompany a Han princess to the Xiongnu court under Emperor Wen. He initially refused, warning 'If you force me to go, I will become a disaster for Han.'

**After Defection:** He became trusted advisor to the Chanyu and worked to preserve Xiongnu cultural independence:

**Against Han Goods:** He warned that Xiongnu strength came from not relying on Han goods. If Xiongnu became fond of Han products, they would become dependent. He demonstrated that Han silk and cotton clothing tore easily in grass and thorns, unlike Xiongnu fur, and that Han food was inferior to Xiongnu dairy products.

**Administrative Reforms:** He taught the Xiongnu to keep written records and count their population and livestock.

**Diplomatic Protocol:** He had the Chanyu use larger tablets (1.2 chi vs Han's 1.1 chi) and more arrogant language in correspondence, signing as 'Heaven-and-Earth-born, Sun-and-Moon-placed Great Chanyu of the Xiongnu.'

**Cultural Defense:** When Han envoys criticized Xiongnu customs (disrespecting elders, marrying stepmothers), he defended them as practical adaptations to nomadic life that ensured survival and family continuity.
