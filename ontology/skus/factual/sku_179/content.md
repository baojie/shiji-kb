While at Tao, Fan Li's middle son killed someone and was imprisoned in Chu. Fan Li said 'Killing someone warrants death, but I've heard that a son of a thousand-gold family doesn't die in the marketplace.' He prepared 1,000 yi of gold in a rough container to send his younger son. The eldest son insisted on going, threatening suicide when refused. Fan Li reluctantly sent him with a letter to his friend Zhuang Sheng, instructing him to give the gold and follow all instructions without argument. Zhuang Sheng, though poor, was respected by the Chu king. He planned to return the gold after securing a pardon. However, the eldest son, not understanding this, stayed and bribed other officials. When he heard a pardon was coming, he retrieved his gold from Zhuang Sheng. Feeling humiliated, Zhuang Sheng told the king that people were saying the pardon was only for Tao Zhu Gong's son. The king ordered the son executed, then issued the pardon. The eldest son returned with his brother's corpse. Fan Li said he expected this: the eldest son, who suffered through hard times, valued money too much; the younger son, born into wealth, would have spent it freely.
