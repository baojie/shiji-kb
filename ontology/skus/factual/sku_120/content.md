## Taigong Wang (太公望) - Lü Shang (吕尚)

### Origins
- **Birthplace**: East Sea region (东海上人)
- **Original surname**: Jiang (姜)
- **Ancestors**: Served as "Four Yue" (四岳), assisted Yu the Great in flood control
- **Fief history**: Ancestors enfeoffed at Lü (吕) or Shen (申) during Yu-Xia period
- **Name origin**: Took Lü from his fief, thus called Lü Shang

### Meeting with King Wen of Zhou
- Was elderly and impoverished, fishing when he met King Wen (then Western Earl/Zhou Xi Bo)
- King Wen's divination predicted: "What is caught is neither dragon nor tiger, but one who will assist a hegemon"
- Meeting location: North bank of Wei River (渭之阳)
- King Wen named him "Taigong Wang" (太公望) meaning "what my grandfather long hoped for"
- Appointed as teacher/advisor to King Wen

### Alternative Accounts of Service
1. **Version 1**: Served King Zhou of Shang, left due to tyranny, wandered among feudal lords, then joined Zhou
2. **Version 2**: Lived as recluse by the sea; recruited by San Yisheng and Hong Yao to help ransom King Wen from imprisonment

### Role in Zhou's Rise
- Helped King Wen with secret strategies to undermine Shang dynasty
- Known for military tactics and cunning plans - later generations regarded him as originator of military strategy
- After King Wen's death, served King Wu as "Shi Shang Fu" (师尚父)
- Led troops at Mengjin with yellow axe and white banner
- **Battle of Muye (11th year, 1st month, jiazi day)**: Defeated Shang army; King Zhou fled to Deer Terrace and was killed
- Distributed wealth from Deer Terrace and grain from Juqiao to relieve the poor
