## Sima Qian (司马迁)
- **Family**: Descendant of historian-officials; father Sima Tan was Grand Historian
- **Birth**: Born at Longmen; herded sheep and farmed as youth
- **Education**: Could recite ancient texts by age 10
- **Travels**: At 20, toured Jiang-Huai, Kuaiji, Jiuyi, Yuan-Xiang, Wen-Si, Qi-Lu, Poyang, Pengcheng
- **Positions**: Palace Gentleman; sent on mission to Ba-Shu and southwestern regions

## The Shiji (Records of the Grand Historian)
- **Father's deathbed request**: Sima Tan urged him to continue the historical work
- **Composition**: Began after becoming Grand Historian in 104 BCE
- **Structure**: 130 chapters - 12 Basic Annals, 10 Tables, 8 Treatises, 30 Hereditary Houses, 70 Biographies
- **Total length**: 526,500 characters
- **Time span**: From Yellow Emperor to Emperor Wu's Taichu era (c. 2600 BCE - 100 BCE)

## The Li Ling Incident
- **Cause**: Defended General Li Ling who surrendered to Xiongnu
- **Punishment**: Sentenced to death; chose castration instead to complete his work
- **Philosophy**: Cited examples of great works written in adversity: King Wen's I Ching, Confucius's Spring and Autumn, Qu Yuan's Li Sao, Sun Bin's Art of War, Lü Buwei's Lüshi Chunqiu, Han Fei's works
- **Purpose**: "To investigate the relationship between heaven and humanity, to understand the changes of past and present, to establish my own school of thought"
