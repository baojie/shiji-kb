During King Wuqiang's reign, Yue attacked Qi in the north and Chu in the west, competing for dominance with central states. During King Wei of Chu's time, Yue attacked Qi in the north. A Qi envoy persuaded Yue to attack Chu instead, arguing that Yue's strategy of waiting for Jin's help was flawed. Yue then attacked Chu. King Wei of Chu counterattacked, decisively defeating Yue, killing King Wuqiang, and taking all former Wu lands up to the Zhe River, while also defeating Qi at Xuzhou. Yue fragmented, with various princes competing for the throne, some becoming kings, others lords, living along the south bank of the Yangtze and the coast, submitting to Chu. Seven generations later, Min Jun Yao helped the feudal lords overthrow the Qin. Han Emperor Gaozu made Yao the King of Yue to continue the Yue line. The Eastern Yue and Min Jun were his descendants.
