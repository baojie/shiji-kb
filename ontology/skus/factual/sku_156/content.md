## Song State Origins

Song was established for descendants of the Shang (Yin) dynasty. After the rebellion of Wu Geng was suppressed, the Duke of Zhou appointed Wei Zi Kai (微子开) to continue the Yin sacrifices, establishing him at Song with the title "Wei Zi's Mandate" (微子之命).

Wei Zi Kai was the eldest son of Emperor Yi and elder half-brother of King Zhou of Shang. He was known for his benevolence and wisdom, and the remaining Yin people loved him.

The state maintained Shang cultural traditions. Duke Xiang of Song (宋襄公) practiced the old Shang virtue of ritual propriety, refusing to attack enemies who were not yet formed for battle at the Battle of Hong (泓之战), which led to his defeat.

The state lasted until King Yan (王偃), called "Jie Song" (桀宋) for his tyranny, was destroyed by the combined forces of Qi, Wei, and Chu in the 47th year of his reign.
