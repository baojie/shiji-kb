**Decline of the Wei Clan (卫氏)**

From the rise of the Wei clan with Wei Qing's first enfeoffment, five marquisates were created among the clan branches. Within 24 years, all five titles were stripped, and no Wei clan member held a marquisate.

- **Wei Kang (伉)**: Eldest son of Wei Qing, Marquis of Yichun, lost title for legal violation
- **Wei Buyi (不疑)**: Son of Wei Qing, Marquis of Yin'an, lost title for酎金 (improper ritual wine gold)
- **Wei Deng (登)**: Son of Wei Qing, Marquis of Fagan, lost title for 酎金
- **Wei Qing**: Died 14 years after the battle against Shanyu, posthumous title Lie Hou
- **Wei Kang**: Inherited Changping marquisate, lost title 6 years later for legal violation

**Reason for no further campaigns**: After the great desert crossing, Han horses were scarce; the empire was focused on conquering Nanyue in the south, Korea in the east, and Qiang and Southwest Yi peoples.
