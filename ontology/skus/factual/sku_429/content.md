## Zhu Jia (朱家)
- **Origin**: Lu
- **Reputation**: Famous for knight-errantry despite Lu's Confucian tradition
- **Achievements**: Saved over 100 prominent men; countless commoners
- **Character**: Never boasted of his deeds; avoided those he helped
- **Lifestyle**: No surplus wealth; simple food and clothing; rode an ox-cart
- **Famous deed**: Secretly saved Ji Bu from execution; never saw him again after Ji became prominent

## Ju Meng (剧孟)
- **Origin**: Luoyang
- **Reputation**: Famous throughout the states
- **Significance**: When Marquis of Tiao led army against Wu-Chu rebellion, finding Ju Meng in Henan was like gaining a rival state
- **Death**: Family had less than 10 gold of wealth

## Guo Xie (郭解)
- **Origin**: Zhi; grandson of physiognomist Xu Fu
- **Father**: Executed for knight-errantry under Emperor Wen
- **Youth**: Killed many; committed robbery, forgery, grave-robbing
- **Reformation**: In later years, practiced virtue, forgave enemies, gave generously
- **Power**: People would voluntarily avenge his slights; settled disputes that local mediators couldn't
- **Downfall**: Forced to move to Maoling despite poverty; his nephew killed the official who reported him
- **Death**: Executed along with his entire clan for crimes committed by his followers without his knowledge
