## Tian Zifang's Teaching on Pride

**Context:** Prince Ji (son of Marquis Wen, later Marquis Wu) met Tian Zifang, his father's teacher, at Chaoge. Prince Ji pulled his carriage aside and bowed, but Tian Zifang did not return the courtesy.

**Prince Ji's Question:**
"Do the rich and noble look down on others, or do the poor and lowly look down on others?"

**Tian Zifang's Response:**
> "It is the poor and lowly who can afford to look down on others. If a feudal lord looks down on others, he will lose his state. If a great officer looks down on others, he will lose his clan. As for the poor and lowly, if their conduct does not accord and their words are not employed, they can simply leave for Chu or Yue, as easily as slipping off a shoe. How can they be treated the same?"

Prince Ji left displeased.
