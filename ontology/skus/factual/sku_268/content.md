**Grand Historian's Assessment**: Pingyuan Lord was a fine nobleman (翩翩浊世之佳公子) of a chaotic age, but lacked big-picture vision. The saying "profit confuses wisdom" (利令智昏) applies - his greed for Feng Ting's evil advice caused Zhao to lose over 400,000 troops at Changping, nearly destroying Handan. Yu Qing was skilled at analyzing situations and planning for Zhao. But his inability to abandon Wei Qi led to his downfall in Daliang. Yet without his poverty and distress, he would not have written books to be known by later generations.
