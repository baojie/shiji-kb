## Duke Huan's Death and Succession Crisis

### Duke Huan's Wives and Sons
**Three principal wives** (no children):
- Wang Ji (王姬)
- Xu Ji (徐姬)
- Cai Ji (蔡姬)

**Six favored consorts** (如夫人) and their sons:
| Consort | Son | Later Title |
|---------|-----|-------------|
| Elder Wei Ji (长卫姬) | Wu Gui (无诡) | No posthumous title |
| Younger Wei Ji (少卫姬) | Yuan (元) | Duke Hui (惠公) |
| Zheng Ji (郑姬) | Zhao (昭) | Duke Xiao (孝公) |
| Ge Ying (葛嬴) | Pan (潘) | Duke Zhao (昭公) |
| Mi Ji (密姬) | Shangren (商人) | Duke Yi (懿公) |
| Song Huazi (宋华子) | Yong (雍) | Prince Yong |

### Original Succession Plan
- Duke Huan and Guan Zhong entrusted Crown Prince Zhao to Duke Xiang of Song
- Yong Wu (易牙), favored by Wei Gong Ji, gained influence and convinced Duke Huan to promise throne to Wu Gui

### Death and Chaos
- **Duke Huan died**: Winter, 10th month, yi-hai day, 43rd year
- Five princes each built factions to compete for throne
- Palace was empty, no one dared to coffin the body
- **Body left unburied for 67 days**, corpse worms came out the door
- 12th month, yi-hai day: Wu Gui was enthroned, then coffined and announced

### Succession After Wu Gui
1. **Wu Gui**: 3 months, killed by Song army; no posthumous title
2. **Duke Xiao (Zhao)**: Enthroned by Song army after defeating four princes' forces
3. **Duke Zhao (Pan)**: Killed Duke Xiao's son with help of Wei prince Kaifang
4. **Duke Yi (Shangren)**: Killed Duke Zhao's son She at the tomb
5. **Duke Hui (Yuan)**: Welcomed from Wei after Duke Yi was assassinated

### Guan Zhong's Final Warning
Before death, Guan Zhong warned against three men:
- **Yi Ya (易牙)**: "Killed his own son to please the ruler - against human nature"
- **Kai Fang (开方)**: "Abandoned his parents to serve the ruler - against human nature"
- **Shu Dao (竖刀)**: "Castrated himself to please the ruler - against human nature"
Duke Huan ignored this advice and employed all three, leading to disaster.
