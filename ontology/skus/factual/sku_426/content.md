## Key Confucian Scholars

### Shen Pei Gong (申培公)
- **Origin**: Lu
- **Student of**: Disciple of Confucius's disciples
- **Teaching**: Only taught the Odes (Shi Jing); no commentary on doubtful passages
- **Students**: Over 100 from distant places; more than 10 became doctors

### Yuan Gu Sheng (辕固生)
- **Origin**: Qi
- **Debate**: Argued with Huang Sheng before Emperor Jing about whether Tang and Wu received the Mandate of Heaven or committed regicide
- **Incident**: Empress Dowager Dou made him fight a boar for calling Laozi "common words"; Emperor Jing gave him a sharp weapon to kill it
- **Age**: Over 90 when recalled during Emperor Wu's reign

### Han Sheng (韩生)
- **Origin**: Yan
- **Work**: Wrote Inner and Outer Commentary on the Odes totaling tens of thousands of words
- **Position**: Grand Tutor to King of Changshan under Emperor Jing

### Fu Sheng (伏生)
- **Origin**: Jinan
- **Position**: Former Qin doctor of letters
- **Age**: Over 90 when Emperor Wen sought him
- **Text preservation**: Hid the Book of Documents in wall during Qin book burning; recovered 29 chapters

### Dong Zhongshu (董仲舒)
- **Origin**: Guangchuan
- **Position**: Doctor of letters under Emperor Jing; Chancellor of Jiangdu under Emperor Wu
- **Study method**: Taught behind curtains for three years without looking at his garden
- **Work**: Expert in Spring and Autumn Annals (Gongyang commentary); wrote on disasters and anomalies
- **Philosophy**: Used yin-yang theory for rain prayers
