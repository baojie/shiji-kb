In the third year, Goujian learned King Fuchai was training troops to attack Yue and decided to strike first, ignoring Fan Li's advice that 'weapons are instruments of misfortune, war violates virtue.' Wu sent elite troops and defeated Yue at Fujiao (夫椒). Goujian retreated with 5,000 remaining soldiers to Huiji (会稽), where Wu besieged them. Goujian sent Minister Wen Zhong (种) to negotiate surrender, offering to become a servant with his wife as a concubine. Despite Wu Zixu's warning that 'Heaven gave Yue to Wu,' King Fuchai accepted the surrender after being influenced by the greedy official Bo Pi (嚭), who was bribed with beauties and treasures.
