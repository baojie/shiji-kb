**Marriage Customs:** When a father died, the son would marry his stepmother. When a brother died, other brothers would marry his widow. This was to prevent the loss of the family line.

**Naming:** They had personal names but no surnames or courtesy names.

**Food Customs:** They ate meat from their livestock and wore leather and fur clothing. The strong ate the best portions, while the elderly ate leftovers. They valued the strong and healthy, and looked down on the old and weak.

**Dwellings:** Fathers and sons shared the same tent.

**Writing:** They had no written documents; agreements were made verbally.
