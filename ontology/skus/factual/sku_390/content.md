**Origins:** After the Baideng siege, Liu Jing (Liu Jing) proposed the Heqin policy to Emperor Gaozu. The Han would send a princess from the imperial family to become the Chanyu's consort, along with annual gifts of silk, wine, and food.

**Terms:** The two states would be like brothers, with annual tribute from Han to Xiongnu.

**Under Empress Dowager Lü:** When Modu sent an insulting letter to Empress Dowager Lü, she wanted to attack but was dissuaded by generals who reminded her that even Emperor Gaozu was trapped at Pingcheng. She continued the Heqin policy.

**Under Emperor Wen:** The policy was renewed multiple times. The Han sent imperial princesses and annual gifts including silk, grain, gold, and other goods.

**Correspondence:** Letters between the Han emperor and Xiongnu Chanyu used formal diplomatic language. The Chanyu's title was 'Heaven-established, Sun-and-Moon-born Great Chanyu of the Xiongnu.'
