# Personnel Evaluation by Humanity Test

## Overview
A method attributed to Guan Zhong for identifying untrustworthy individuals by examining whether their actions violate basic human nature and natural affections.

## Core Principle
People who violate fundamental human bonds to please superiors cannot be trusted. Their extreme actions indicate they seek personal gain at any cost.

## Evaluation Criteria

### Test 1: Parental Bond Test
- **Question**: Has the person harmed their own children to please a superior?
- **Example**: Yi Ya (易牙) killed his own son to cook for the ruler
- **Verdict**: "非人情，不可" (Against human nature, cannot be trusted)

### Test 2: Family Loyalty Test
- **Question**: Has the person abandoned or betrayed their parents/close family for a superior?
- **Example**: Kai Fang (开方) left his parents to serve
- **Verdict**: "非人情，难近" (Against human nature, difficult to approach safely)

### Test 3: Self-Preservation Test
- **Question**: Has the person mutilated themselves to please a superior?
- **Example**: Shu Diao (竖刀) castrated himself to serve in the palace
- **Verdict**: "非人情，难亲" (Against human nature, difficult to trust closely)

## Decision Matrix

| Violation Type | Trust Level | Recommendation |
|---------------|-------------|----------------|
| Harmed child | None | Never appoint |
| Abandoned family | Very Low | Keep at distance |
| Self-mutilation | Low | Do not give power |
| None of above | Evaluate further | Proceed with other tests |

## Expected Outcomes
- Identification of potentially treacherous individuals before they gain power
- Protection of the state from those who lack natural human bonds
- More reliable leadership team
