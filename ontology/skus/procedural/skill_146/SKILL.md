# Trust Securing Through Historical Analogy

Based on Gan Mao's method before attacking Yi Yang.

## Overview
Before undertaking a difficult task where others may undermine you, secure a formal pledge by illustrating how easily trust can be broken.

## Steps

1. **Identify the Risk**
   - Recognize that long campaigns allow time for enemies to slander you
   - Identify who might speak against you (colleagues, rivals)

2. **Select Appropriate Analogy**
   - Choose a story about false accusations believed through repetition
   - Example: Zeng Shen's mother believed he killed someone after three people said so, despite his known virtue

3. **Present Your Case**
   - "My virtue is less than the historical figure"
   - "Your trust in me is less than a mother's trust"
   - "Those who will speak against me are more than three"

4. **Secure Formal Commitment**
   - Request a pledge or oath before proceeding
   - Reference this pledge if challenged later ("Remember [place]!")

5. **Invoke Pledge When Needed**
   - When ruler wavers, remind them of the specific pledge location
   - This forces acknowledgment of original commitment

## Expected Outcomes
- Binding commitment secured
- Protection against later slander
- Ruler reminded of obligation at critical moment
