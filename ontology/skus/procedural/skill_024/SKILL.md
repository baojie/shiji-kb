# Conduct State Sacrifices

## Overview
A procedure for performing official state sacrificial rituals to deities, establishing religious legitimacy. Based on Qin's various sacrificial ceremonies (畤).

## Steps

1. **Select the Occasion**
   - New state establishment
   - Major military victory
   - Regular calendrical observances

2. **Prepare the Altar (畤)**
   - Construct or designate the sacrificial altar
   - Choose appropriate location (e.g., western altar for western regions)

3. **Prepare Sacrificial Animals**
   - Select appropriate animals (horses, cattle, sheep)
   - Standard offering: three of each kind (三牢)
   - Larger ceremonies may use hundreds of animals

4. **Conduct the Ritual**
   - Perform sacrifice according to established rites
   - Make offerings to specified deities (e.g., Supreme God/上帝, White Emperor)

5. **Record the Event**
   - Document the establishment of new ritual traditions
   - Note any omens or divine responses

## Examples
- Duke Xiang established the Western Altar (西畤) using three each of red horses, yellow cattle, and rams
- Duke Wen established the Fu Altar (鄜畤) using three sacrificial animals
- Duke De used 300 sacrificial animals for the Fu Altar

## Expected Outcomes
- Religious legitimacy for the state
- Documentation of state traditions
- Connection between rulership and divine authority
