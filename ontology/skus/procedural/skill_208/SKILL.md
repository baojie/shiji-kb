# Soldier-Centered Leadership

## Overview
A leadership philosophy that emphasizes sharing hardships with subordinates, distributing rewards equitably, and prioritizing soldier welfare to build deep loyalty and willingness to follow into danger.

## Core Principles

### 1. Share Rewards and Resources
- Distribute all rewards and gifts among subordinates
- Share food and drink equally with soldiers
- Maintain no personal wealth accumulation

### 2. Share Hardships
- Do not drink water until all soldiers have drunk
- Do not eat until all soldiers have eaten
- Endure the same conditions as subordinates

### 3. Simplified Command Structure
- Reduce formal military bureaucracy
- Minimize paperwork and administrative requirements
- Allow soldiers flexibility in camp arrangements
- Camp near good water and pasture when possible

### 4. Lead by Example
- Demonstrate personal courage in battle
- Take personal risks alongside soldiers
- Show calmness under pressure

### 5. Exercise Restraint
- Be lenient rather than harsh in discipline
- Avoid excessive strictness
- Trust subordinates' judgment

## Expected Outcomes
- Soldiers will be willing to die for their commander
- High morale and unit cohesion
- Deep loyalty that persists through difficult campaigns
- Subordinates will follow willingly into dangerous situations
