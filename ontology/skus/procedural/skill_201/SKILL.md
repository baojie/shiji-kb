## Overview
A persuasion technique for changing a superior's hostile stance by combining emotional appeal with evidence of loyalty and contribution.

## Steps
1. **Identify the grievance**: Understand exactly why the superior is displeased
2. **Find an intermediary**: Approach someone close to the superior (e.g., a trusted relative) rather than directly confronting the superior
3. **Lead with emotion**: Begin with tears or visible distress to signal sincerity and gravity
4. **Reframe the narrative**: Present the accused party's actions in context:
   - Cite specific past contributions and sacrifices
   - Explain apparent transgressions as misunderstandings or justified actions
   - Highlight the accused's fundamental loyalty and filial piety
5. **Invoke shared bonds**: Emphasize family relationships and the superior's own role in the situation
6. **Show vulnerability**: Describe the accused's fear, regret, and suffering to evoke sympathy
7. **Request advocacy**: Ask the intermediary to speak on behalf of the accused

## Decision Points
- If the grievance involves formal protocol violations, explain them as demonstrations of the superior's favor rather than arrogance
- If the superior feels disrespected, frame the accused's actions as attempts to honor the superior

## Expected Outcomes
- The superior's anger transforms into understanding
- Relations are restored and potentially strengthened
- The advocate gains reputation and rewards
