# Strategic Retreat Decision Framework

A decision-making process for balancing honor against tactical advantage in warfare.

## Overview
When facing an enemy with whom you have a past agreement, use this framework to decide whether to advance or retreat.

## Decision Criteria

### Honor-Based Considerations
- Past verbal agreements or promises made
- Reputation for keeping one's word
- Long-term trust with other states
- Moral standing among allies

### Tactical Considerations
- Current military position
- Enemy strength and morale
- Terrain advantages
- Strategic objectives

## Decision Process

1. **Recall Past Agreements**: Review any promises made to the opposing party
2. **Assess Current Situation**: Evaluate military position and odds of victory
3. **Weigh Long-term Impact**: Consider reputation vs. immediate gain
4. **Make Decision**:
   - If agreement exists and honor is paramount → Retreat as promised
   - If no agreement or honor already compromised → Pursue tactical advantage
5. **Document Reasoning**: Record decision rationale for future reference

## Example Application
When Duke Wen faced Chu forces, he retreated three stages (三舍) as promised during his exile, despite tactical advantage. This demonstrated honor and built long-term credibility.

## Expected Outcome
A decision that balances immediate military needs with long-term reputation and alliance maintenance.
