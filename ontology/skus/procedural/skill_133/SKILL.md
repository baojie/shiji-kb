# Reputation Preservation Through Association

## Overview
A method for understanding how individuals achieve lasting fame, based on the principle that reputation endures through connection with renowned figures.

## Core Principle
"闾巷之人，欲砥行立名者，非附青云之士，恶能施于后世哉？"
(Ordinary people who wish to establish their names must attach themselves to eminent persons)

## Steps

1. **Cultivate Personal Virtue**
   - Practice moral conduct (砥行)
   - Develop noteworthy character and achievements

2. **Seek Association with Eminent Figures**
   - Connect with recognized sages, scholars, or leaders
   - Position oneself in relation to their legacy (附骥尾 - attaching to the horse's tail)

3. **Document the Connection**
   - Ensure the association is recorded in texts or traditions
   - Example: Bo Yi and Shu Qi became famous through Confucius's mention

## Examples
- Bo Yi & Shu Qi: Unknown without Confucius's endorsement
- Yan Hui: Fame enhanced through association with Confucius

## Expected Outcomes
- Greater likelihood of name surviving in historical record
- Enhanced reputation through reflected prestige
- Legacy extending beyond one's immediate era
