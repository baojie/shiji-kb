# Zhou Yi Divination Interpretation

## Overview
This method describes the interpretation of Zhou Yi (I Ching) hexagrams for divination, as demonstrated in the case of Chen Li Gong divining about his son.

## Procedure

### Step 1: Perform the Divination
- Engage a trained diviner (太史)
- Conduct the milfoil stalk divination
- Record the resulting hexagram and any changing lines

### Step 2: Identify the Hexagram Transformation
- Note the original hexagram (本卦)
- Note the transformed hexagram (之卦)
- Example: 观之否 (Guan changing to Pi)

### Step 3: Analyze the Hexagram Statement
- Read the relevant line texts
- Example: "观国之光，利用宾于王" (Observing the glory of the state, beneficial to serve as guest to the king)

### Step 4: Apply Interpretive Framework

**Location of Fulfillment**:
- Will it manifest here (此国)?
- Or in another state (异国)?

**Timing of Fulfillment**:
- In this person's lifetime (此身)?
- Or in descendants (子孙)?

**Clan Affiliation**:
- Identify the ruling clan of the future state
- Example: 姜姓 (Jiang clan) = descendants of Tai Yue

### Step 5: Apply the Principle of Balance
- "物莫能两大" (Things cannot both be great)
- If one state rises, another must decline
- The decline of the original state may enable the rise of descendants elsewhere

## Example Interpretation
For Chen Li Gong's son:
- Hexagram: Guan changing to Pi
- Meaning: Will serve in royal court, but not in Chen
- Timing: Will manifest in descendants
- Location: A Jiang-ruled state (later fulfilled in Qi)

## Expected Outcomes
- Understanding of potential future trajectories
- Guidance for strategic decisions
- Insight into generational patterns
