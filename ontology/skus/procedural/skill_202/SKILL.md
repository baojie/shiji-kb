## Overview
A method for persuading a superior to make painful decisions during a crisis by using historical analogies, emotional appeal, and consequence analysis.

## Steps
1. **Express personal accountability**: Begin by offering to accept punishment or death for the situation, demonstrating loyalty and seriousness
2. **Use historical analogies**: Compare the current situation to historical precedents:
   - Select cases involving similar relationships but severe consequences
   - Show that even the closest family bonds did not prevent enforcement of law/protocol
3. **Invoke proverbs**: Use well-known sayings that capture the danger of the situation
4. **Identify the core conflict**: Explain the tension between personal affection and public duty
5. **Create urgency about the future**: Point out what will happen if the current protector (the ultimate authority figure) is no longer present
6. **Offer a clear resolution**: Specify exactly what action must be taken

## Key Principles
- "主辱臣死" (When the lord is humiliated, the minister should die) - accept responsibility first
- "治天下终不以私乱公" (Governing the world never allows private matters to disorder public ones)
- Use the superior's fear of future isolation as motivation

## Expected Outcomes
- The superior recognizes the necessity of action
- Difficult decisions are made with emotional support
- The advisor's standing increases due to demonstrated loyalty and wisdom
