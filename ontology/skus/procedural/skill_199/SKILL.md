## Overview
This procedure outlines how to advance one's career when competing against more established figures for leadership positions, using strategic yielding to gain both position and reputation.

## Steps
1. **Assess the competition** - Identify if a rival has longer-standing prestige and broader support
2. **Calculate equivalence** - Determine if alternative positions offer equal authority (e.g., Chancellor vs. Grand Commandant have equal rank)
3. **Advocate for the rival** - If appointed first, publicly yield to the more established competitor
4. **Accept the alternative position** - Take the position of equal rank that becomes available
5. **Build reputation** - Gain fame for humility and respect for established merit

## Decision Points
- Only yield if the alternative position provides equivalent power and prestige
- The yield must appear genuine, not calculated

## Expected Outcomes
- Secure a position of equal authority
- Build reputation for humility and wisdom
- Avoid direct confrontation with established powers
- Position yourself for future advancement when circumstances change
