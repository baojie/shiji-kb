# Strategic Decision Framework (from debates on conquest priorities)

## Overview
A framework for evaluating strategic options when facing competing priorities.

## The Core Question
When resources are limited, which target should be prioritized?

## Framework Application: Conquer Distant vs. Nearby

### Option A: Attack the Prestigious Target
**Arguments for:**
- Greater fame and legitimacy
- Control of symbolic resources (e.g., nine tripods, maps, archives)
- Ability to command in name of authority

**Risks:**
- May unite enemies against you
- Could be seen as illegitimate aggression
- Might provoke intervention from other powers

### Option B: Attack the Practical Target
**Arguments for:**
- Easier to achieve
- Expands territory and resources
- Builds strength incrementally
- Less likely to provoke coalition against you
- Gains reputation for "stopping chaos" rather than "creating chaos"

**Risks:**
- Less prestige
- May seem like small thinking

## Decision Criteria
1. **Feasibility**: Can we actually succeed?
2. **Consequences**: What reactions will this provoke?
3. **Net Benefit**: Do gains outweigh costs and risks?
4. **Reputation**: How will this be perceived?

## Key Insight
"欲富国者务广其地，欲彊兵者务富其民，欲王者务博其德" - To enrich the state, expand territory; to strengthen the army, enrich the people; to become a true king, cultivate virtue.

## Expected Outcome
A strategic choice that balances ambition with practicality.
