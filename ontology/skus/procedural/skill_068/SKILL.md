## Overview
A protocol for transferring leadership through a designed sequence of succession, ensuring a designated heir ultimately receives the position.

## Steps

1. **Designate the Ultimate Heir**
   - Identify the most worthy successor
   - Secure commitment from current leader
   - Document the intention clearly

2. **Establish Transfer Sequence**
   - Create a chain of succession through brothers
   - Each holder passes to the next in line
   - Sequence ends with designated heir

3. **Build in Safeguards**
   - Make the protocol a binding command
   - Ensure all parties understand the sequence
   - Create social pressure for compliance

4. **Handle Refusal Gracefully**
   - If designated heir refuses, respect their choice
   - Allow them to demonstrate virtue through refusal
   - Return succession to normal lineage pattern

## Example Application
King Shoumeng of Wu's succession plan:
- Desired heir: Ji Zha (youngest, most virtuous)
- Sequence: Shoumeng → Zhufan → Yuji → Yumei → Ji Zha
- Each brother was to pass the throne to the next
- Ji Zha ultimately refused, so Yumei's son took the throne

## Decision Points
- If designated heir consistently refuses, honor their choice
- If protocol is interrupted, follow established precedent
- Balance between protocol and virtue of refusal

## Expected Outcomes
- Orderly transition of power
- Demonstration of virtue through refusal
- Family harmony maintained through clear expectations
